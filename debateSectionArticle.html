When I first read about nesting article or section tags I thought the answer was simple: do not worry about it. Use whatever fits your code! Then I read the three articles on the left that lay down rules for nesting articles inside a section tag or the other way around. The authors made arguments I found compelling and logical and I have definitly changed my opinion! The writers came to the conclusion that the article tag should contain content that is self contained. In other words, content that is not related to other information on the webpage. A section element should be used when grouping content and should contain a heading. When nesting section inside article, section should be used to group content of parent container. For example: a list of comments on a blog. The comments should be inside a section tag and the blog in a article tag. When nesting article inside section, the articles should have self contained content. For example: an article on a topic of a blog. I think that these rules give respect to the meaning and original definiton of the section and article tag. Looking at the definitions of the words it also makes sense why the authors came to their conclusions.
